# Brand voice: GTM Tech Week

Read this before writing anything public. Every post, email, prospectus line and landing block in this
kit follows it. The voice is the product. If a sentence could appear in a generic conference brochure,
delete it.

## One line
Direct, operator to operator, problem first. We sound like someone who builds revenue systems, not
someone who markets to people who build them.

## What the voice is
- **Problem first, not product first.** Open on what is broken, not on what attendees get. "Most
  companies are bolting AI onto a broken revenue motion."
- **Systems and engineering vocabulary.** engine, wired, cadence, discipline, operating system,
  pipeline, motion. GTM is architecture, not vibes.
- **Short declaratives and fragments.** "Not more AI SDR spam." Drop the verb when it hits harder.
- **Contrast structure.** "More tools, more outbound, more activity. Still no scalable system."
  Activity versus outcome is the recurring move.
- **Lists of three.** sales, marketing, ops. strategy, data, process. Stack nouns for momentum.
- **Names the real thing.** "Workshops, roundtables and dinners," not "experiences." Real rooms, real
  roles, real numbers.
- **Mostly sentence case.** Plain, not stylised. The lowercase-ish feel comes from restraint, not from
  forcing everything lowercase.

## What the voice is not
- Not hype. Not "AI will replace your SDRs." Not "10x your pipeline."
- Not corporate. Not aspirational fog.
- Not loud. We do not shout to sound confident.

## Hard rules
- **No em-dashes.** Use a period or a comma. This is the one mechanical tell that separates us from
  agency copy. (Note: this is the opposite of some house styles. Here, em-dashes are banned.)
- **No exclamation points.**
- **No emoji in body copy.** (A single check or arrow in a UI label is fine.)
- **Real numbers, unrounded, with the symbol.** "£295 early bird," not "affordable tickets."
- **Second person for the reader, first-plural for us.** "you / your," "we / us."

## Banned words (the anti-fluff list)
synergy, leverage, unlock, empower, thought leadership, cutting-edge, world-class, revolutionary,
game-changing, next-gen, supercharge, seamless, holistic, ecosystem of solutions, journey, "experience"
(as a noun for the event), transform your business, reimagine, elevate, AI-powered (as hype),
best-in-class, turnkey, frictionless.

If you need one of those words to make the sentence work, the sentence is wrong. Name the mechanism
instead: wire, system, engine, cadence, process, motion.

## The one thing we are against
"AI SDR spam" and activity-theatre. More outbound for its own sake, more tools that do not connect,
scattered AI experiments with no system underneath. Everything we publish is the opposite of that.
