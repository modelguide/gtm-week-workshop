# sources/ — raw imports, kept exactly as they came

> **Workshop TL;DR (for humans):** The permanent home for raw material that *isn't* a meeting — an
> email, an article, a deck. Compile drops it here and **never edits it again.** Your brain's pages point
> back to these as proof. This is the other half of "the inbox emptied but nothing was lost."

---

## What goes here
Non-meeting raw imports: emails, articles, research docs, decks, web clippings. The thing as it arrived.

## What does NOT go here
- A meeting transcript → `meetings/` (kept inline on the meeting page, not here).
- Anything Claude *wrote* (a recommendation, a report) → `agent/`.

## The rule: immutable
Once compile moves a file here, it's **read-only forever.** You read it and cite it; you never rewrite
it. All the thinking it produces lives on the `companies/`/`people/` pages it touched, with a
`[Source: ...]` pointing back here.

## Filename + frontmatter
`<YYYY-MM-DD>-<slug>.md` (the source's own date). Frontmatter: `type: source`,
`source_kind: email | article | research | deck | web`, `date`.
